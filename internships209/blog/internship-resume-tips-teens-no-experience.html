How to Write an Internship Resume With No Work Experience

📅 March 12, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read 📍 For 209 Area Students

The Truth: You Already Have More Than You Think

The biggest mistake students make when writing their first resume is waiting until they "have something to put on it." That moment never comes if you never apply — so let's fix that thinking right now.

Every internship program in the 209 — from the City of Stockton Y.E.S.S. to Gallo to SJC WorkNet — regularly accepts first-time applicants with no prior work experience. They built their programs for exactly that person. What they're looking for isn't a work history. They're looking for effort, availability, and honesty about who you are.

Here's how to build a real resume from scratch.

Resume Structure for a First-Timer

Your resume should be one page, no exceptions. Here's the order of sections:

  1. Header — your name, phone, email, city
  2. Objective or Summary — 2 sentences about who you are and what you want
  3. Education — school, expected graduation, GPA (if 3.0 or higher)
  4. Skills — hard skills and soft skills
  5. Activities, Clubs & Volunteer Work — this replaces "Work Experience" until you have some
  6. Coursework — relevant classes (optional but helpful)

That's it. Now let's go section by section.

📌 Section 1: Header

Put your name at the top in larger text (16–18pt). Below it: your phone number, email address, and city (you don't need a street address).

Example
MARIA GARCIA (209) 555-0192 · maria.garcia@email.com · Stockton, CA

Important: Use a professional email — first.lastname@gmail.com is fine. Not "xoxodancer99@hotmail.com."

🎯 Section 2: Objective Statement

Two sentences max. Say what you are, what you're applying for, and what you bring. Be specific to the program you're applying to — don't use a generic statement.

Generic (weak)
Seeking an internship opportunity to gain experience in a professional environment.
Specific (strong)
Junior at Edison High School in Stockton with a 3.4 GPA and strong interest in public service. Seeking a summer position with the City of Stockton Y.E.S.S. Program to contribute to community operations while developing professional skills.

🎓 Section 3: Education

This is your main section until you have work experience. Include:

  • School name and city
  • Diploma / expected graduation year
  • GPA — only list it if it's 3.0 or higher. Leave it off if it's lower, no one will ask.
  • Honors or relevant awards (Honor Roll, AP classes, etc.)
Example
Edison High School — Stockton, CA Expected Graduation: June 2027 GPA: 3.4 / 4.0 Honors: Honor Roll (Fall 2025, Spring 2025), AP Environmental Science

⚡ Section 4: Skills

Split into two types: hard skills (things you know how to do technically) and soft skills (how you work with people).

Example
Technical: Microsoft Word, Excel, Google Docs, Google Sheets, Canva, basic Python (from CS class) Languages: Bilingual — English and Spanish (read, write, speak) Soft Skills: Punctual, works well in teams, comfortable speaking to adults

Bilingual is a superpower in the 209. If you speak Spanish and English fluently, put that at the top of your skills section — it is actively valued by every employer from the City of Stockton to WorkNet to healthcare organizations.

🌟 Section 5: Activities, Clubs & Volunteer Work

This is the most underused section by first-time applicants. If you've never been paid for work, this section does the heavy lifting. Include:

  • Sports teams (any position, any level)
  • School clubs: ASB, debate, robotics, DECA, FFA, 4-H, theater, choir, band
  • Volunteer work: church, food banks, neighborhood cleanups, tutoring younger students
  • Informal work: babysitting, lawn mowing, helping at a family business, selling things online
  • Community service hours required by school (if 20+ hours)
How to write each entry
ASB Secretary — Edison High School (2024–Present) Coordinate communications between student body and administration for school of 1,800 students. Managed event logistics for Homecoming 2025 (attendance: 400+). Volunteer — Second Harvest Food Bank, Stockton (Summer 2025) Sorted and distributed food to 200+ families per shift. Worked alongside 10-person team every Saturday for 8 weeks.

Notice how each entry has: a title, where/when, and what you actually did + a number. Numbers make things real. How many people? How many weeks? How many events?

📚 Section 6: Relevant Coursework (Optional)

If you're applying to a specific type of internship, list 3–5 classes that relate to it. This shows employers you have foundational knowledge, even without experience.

Example — applying to a healthcare internship
Relevant Coursework: Biology (A), Anatomy & Physiology (A-), Health Science Careers (A), Statistics
Example — applying to a government/policy internship
Relevant Coursework: AP Government (A), Economics, English Composition, Spanish II

Common Mistakes to Avoid

✅ Do this

  • Use a simple, clean format — no fancy colors or graphics
  • Use the same font throughout (Arial, Calibri, or Georgia)
  • Proofread everything twice
  • Save as PDF before submitting
  • Name your file: FirstLast_Resume.pdf

❌ Avoid this

  • Don't use "Objective: To gain experience" — be specific
  • Don't list every class you've ever taken
  • Don't include a photo — not standard in the US
  • Don't go past one page
  • Don't use Comic Sans or any decorative fonts

Writing a Cover Letter (Yes, You Need One)

Many students skip the cover letter because it's optional. That's exactly why you should write one — it immediately separates you from everyone who didn't. Keep it short: three paragraphs, under half a page.

📝 Cover Letter Formula

Paragraph 1 — Who you are and what you want
I'm a junior at Edison High School in Stockton applying for the Y.E.S.S. summer internship program. I'm interested in working in the City's Parks and Recreation or IT department.
Paragraph 2 — Why you'd be good at this (use your activities/skills)
I currently serve as ASB Secretary, where I've coordinated communication between student leadership and school administration. I'm comfortable with spreadsheets and Google Workspace, and I've volunteered at Second Harvest Food Bank for two summers — so I understand what showing up consistently looks like.
Paragraph 3 — Close
I'd welcome the opportunity to contribute to Stockton's city operations this summer. Thank you for your time and consideration. Sincerely, Maria Garcia

Where to Build Your Resume for Free

You don't need to buy anything. Use one of these free tools:

  • Google Docs — search "resume template" in the template gallery. Clean, professional, free. This is the best option.
  • Canva — free templates at canva.com/resumes. Great if you want something that looks slightly more polished.
  • Microsoft Word — available on any school computer; has built-in templates.

Once you're done, download as PDF. Never send a .docx file to an employer — formatting can break on their end.

Final Pre-Send Checklist

  • Spell your own name correctly (it happens)
  • Phone number is correct and has voicemail set up
  • Email address is professional and you actually check it
  • GPA listed only if 3.0 or higher
  • At least one activity or volunteer experience listed
  • Objective statement is specific to this program
  • No spelling errors (read it backwards — it forces you to see each word)
  • Saved as PDF, file named: YourName_Resume.pdf
  • Cover letter attached (or pasted into email body if submitting by email)

Now: Go Apply

The resume is done. The only thing left is to actually submit it. The 209's best programs — Y.E.S.S., WorkNet, Merced Youth Jobs, Stanislaus County, and dozens of others — are actively looking for motivated students right now. Most spring deadlines hit in March and April.
