Summer Jobs for 16-Year-Olds in Stockton & the Central Valley (2026)

📅 March 12, 2026 ⏱ 7 min read 📍 Stockton, Modesto, Merced & the 209

What You Need to Know First: Work Permits at 16

If you're 16 in California and want a job this summer, here's the most important thing: you need a work permit before you can legally start working. It's called a "Statement of Intent to Employ a Minor," and both you and your employer need to get it from your school. It sounds complicated — it isn't. The whole process takes about a week.

Here's the step-by-step:

  1. Get the form (B1-1) from your school's attendance office or counseling center. Most schools have it at the front desk. You can also download it from the California Department of Education website.
  2. Have your employer fill out their section — they'll put in the job title, hours, and their signature. You need this before your school will approve anything.
  3. Bring the form back to school for your principal's signature. They'll check that you're in good standing (attendance, grades).
  4. Get your permit — the school issues it and keeps a copy. Your employer keeps the original on file. You can start work.

If you're applying to programs like the City of Stockton Y.E.S.S. or San Joaquin County internships, list your school's name on applications — they'll ask about it during onboarding.

How Many Hours Can You Work at 16?

California has specific rules to protect student workers. During summer and school breaks, 16 and 17-year-olds can work up to 8 hours per day and 48 hours per week. You also can't work before 5am or after 10pm. During the school year, it drops to 4 hours on school days.

Quick tip: Summer programs like Y.E.S.S. are specifically designed around these rules — most cap hours at 20–30 per week so you stay within the legal limit and still have time to be 16.

What If You're Not in Stockton?

Good news: every major city in the 209 has options for 16-year-olds.

Modesto: The City of Modesto internship program is open to high school students. WorkNet of Stanislaus County runs youth employment programs similar to SJC WorkNet. Stanislaus County government also hires student interns year-round.

Merced: The City of Merced Youth Jobs Program places teens 16+ in paid work experience with local employers across retail, healthcare, culinary, and public service. mercedyouthjobs.com is where to start.

Turlock & Surrounding Areas: Stanislaus County Summer Youth Employment and private employers like Foster Farms and Hilmar Cheese often hire 16+ for entry-level summer positions.

Private Sector Jobs Open to 16-Year-Olds

Beyond government programs, local businesses hire 16-year-olds in the 209 every summer. These aren't "internships" in the formal sense, but they count as real work experience:

  • Retail and food service: Target, Grocery Outlet, In-N-Out, local restaurants. Easy to get in summer, transfers well to resume.
  • Customer service: Banks, insurance offices, and local businesses often hire high school students for summer admin support.
  • Agriculture: The Central Valley is ag country. Farm work through labor contractors is available to 16+ with parental permission. See our ag internship guide for structured programs.
  • Parks & recreation: City parks departments in Stockton, Modesto, and Merced often hire teen program aides for summer camps.

How to Stand Out When You're 16 and Have Zero Experience

Every employer hiring a 16-year-old knows you have no experience. That's expected. What they're actually looking for is:

  • Reliability — show up on time, communicate clearly
  • Attitude — enthusiasm and willingness to learn beats a polished resume
  • A complete application — fill out every field, write a real cover letter (even a short one), and double-check your email address

The biggest mistake 16-year-olds make is submitting half-finished applications. If a program asks for a resume and you've never worked before, write one anyway — list your school, GPA, extracurriculars, volunteer work, and any skills (even babysitting counts). Deadlines: Don't Wait

Most summer programs in the 209 close applications in March and April. The Y.E.S.S. Program, San Joaquin County internships, and Tracy's program all fill up early. If you're reading this in March — apply this week, not next week.

Programs that are "rolling" (open year-round) are less urgent, but applying early still puts you at the front of the list. WorkNet and Merced Youth Jobs accept applications anytime.
