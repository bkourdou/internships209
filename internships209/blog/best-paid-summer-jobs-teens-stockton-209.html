Best Paid Summer Jobs for Teens in Stockton & the 209 (2026)

📅 March 3, 2026 ⏱ 6 min read 📍 Stockton, Modesto, Merced & the 209

Why Summer 2026 is Your Best Chance to Get Hired

If you're a teen looking to earn real money this summer, you're reading this at the perfect time. Here's the thing: most summer programs open applications in January and February, and they close them in March and April. That means your window to apply is right now.

In the 209 area (Stockton, Modesto, Merced, Turlock, and Tracy), there are dozens of paid internships, jobs, and work-study programs specifically designed for high school and college students. Some pay $23–$30/hour. Some offer stipends of $2,000–$3,500. And most are way less competitive than you'd think — especially if you apply before March 31.

Let's break down the best opportunities, what they pay, who can apply, and how to land one.

The Biggest Paid Opportunities in the 209

City of Stockton Y.E.S.S. Program

What it is: Youth Employment Support Service — a city-funded program that hires local high school and college students for paid summer internships across Stockton.

Pay Rate
$23/hour
Ages
16–19
Spots
~100
Duration
8–10 weeks

Positions: Interns work in city departments — Parks & Rec, Planning, IT, Community Services. It's real work that actually matters.

deadline is in early March — check internships209.com/listings.html for the current date.gov for the current application deadline and link).

Why apply: You'll get job experience *and* a paycheck. Plus, working for the city looks amazing on college applications.

San Joaquin County WorkNet

What it is: The county's workforce development program with WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act) youth funding. They run job training and paid work placements.

Ages
14–24
Pay
Varies + Stipends
Duration
8–12 weeks

What they offer: Paid internships in retail, healthcare, tech, and manufacturing. Many positions come with stipends (cash bonuses) for completing training or work hours.

Deadline: Rolling, but apply by end of March for best placement.

How to apply: Visit sjcworknet.org or call to speak with a youth counselor who'll match you with a job.

Opportunities in Modesto & Stanislaus County

Gallo Winery Internships

E. & J. Gallo (one of the world's largest wineries) runs a summer internship program for college students. Positions include operations, marketing, sales, and production.

  • Pay: $26–$30/hour
  • Duration: 10–12 weeks
  • Who: College students (some high school juniors/seniors with strong GPA)
  • How to apply: Check gallo.com/careers for internship postings

Bay Valley Tech Bootcamp

A local tech training program that partners with employers to train and hire interns in coding, data, and IT support.

  • Pay: $25/hour (during and after bootcamp)
  • Duration: 8-week bootcamp + potential job placement
  • Who: High school seniors and college students
  • Cost: Often free or low-cost (ask about scholarships)

Opportunities in Merced & Merced County

Merced Youth Jobs Program

Merced County runs a dedicated youth employment program with work-study and paid internship placements.

  • Stipend: Up to $3,000
  • Ages: 14–22
  • Duration: 6–8 weeks
  • Who: Priority given to low-income students and first-time job seekers

UC Merced Summer Research Opportunities

UC Merced offers paid summer research positions for high school students interested in STEM, engineering, and social sciences.

  • Pay: Up to $3,500 for the summer
  • Duration: 8 weeks, full-time
  • Who: High school juniors and seniors with GPA 3.0+
  • Application: Check ucmerced.edu for "Summer Research" or "STEM Pathways"

How to Actually Get Hired: Application Tips

1. Apply EARLY (We Mean It)

Most programs have rolling deadlines but close applications in late March or early April. If you wait until May or June, you'll miss out. Apply in the next 2 weeks.

2. Have Your Work Permit Ready

If you're under 18 in California, you need a work permit (AB 1275 or school-issued). Get this from your school or employer ASAP. It's free and usually takes 1–2 days.

Not sure how? Check our Work Permit Guide →

3. Write a Simple Cover Email

You don't need a fancy cover letter. A short email will do. Here's a template:

Subject: Summer Intern Application — [Your Name]

Hi [Program/Hiring Manager Name],

I'm [Name], a [10th/11th/12th grade OR college year] student at [School], and I'm interested in the [Position] internship this summer. I'm eager to gain real work experience and contribute to your team. I'm available [dates] and have reliable transportation.

Thanks for considering my application. I look forward to hearing from you.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Phone]

4. Fill Out Applications Fully

Don't skip sections. If a form asks for GPA, grades, or a short essay, answer everything. Half-finished applications get rejected automatically.

5. Follow Up (But Not Too Much)

Applied 2 weeks ago and haven't heard back? Send a polite follow-up email: *"Hi, I applied for [position] on [date] and wanted to check on my application status. Thanks!"* — but don't spam them.

The Bottom Line

Summer 2026 has real money waiting for you — $23–$30/hour is solid for a teen job. The Stockton Y.E.S.S. program alone has 100+ spots. WorkNet, Gallo, UC Merced, and Merced County are all actively hiring right now.

Your only job is to apply. Today.
