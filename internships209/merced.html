Merced's Growing Opportunity Ecosystem

Merced County is home to UC Merced — the youngest University of California campus and a growing hub of research and innovation in the Central Valley. The university offers multiple paid internship programs for enrolled undergrads, including the Student Success Internship (up to $3,500 stipend) and a Community & Labor Center summer placement focused on Central Valley organizing.

For high school students and community college students, the Merced Youth Jobs Program (ages 16–24, City of Merced) provides paid work experience with local employers, a personal mentor, and up to $3,000 in earnings over 200 hours. Merced College's Workplace Internship Network (WIN) connects CTE students to 90-day paid placements with regional employers.

Hilmar Cheese Company — one of the world's largest dairy processors, located just outside Merced in Hilmar — offers competitive 10-week summer internships in engineering, IT, food science, quality, and finance. With strong employer diversity and a community-minded workforce development ecosystem, Merced offers more opportunities than its size might suggest.

Frequently Asked Questions — Merced Internships

However, other Merced-area programs like the Merced Youth Jobs Program and Merced College WIN are open to all local students regardless of where they attend school.

Hilmar Cheese is one of the world's largest dairy processors, located in Hilmar (Merced County). Their 10-week summer internships are competitive and open to students who have completed their sophomore year in relevant fields like Engineering, Food Science, IT, Finance, or Quality Assurance. Interns work on real projects and attend dairy tours and leadership sessions.

The Workplace Internship Network (WIN) at Merced College connects students who complete CTE (Career and Technical Education) Fast Track programs to 90-day paid internships with regional employers. Successful interns may be considered for full-time hire after their internship ends. Participants must be enrolled Merced College students who complete the required workshops.
