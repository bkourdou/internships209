Built for the 209.
By someone who lives it every day.

My name is Baris Kourdou. I'm a Student Services Coordinator at the California Department of Rehabilitation, based in Turlock. I built Internships209 because I see the gap from the inside — and I decided to do something about it.

Why I Built This

In my day job at the California Department of Rehabilitation, I work directly with young people — ages 16 to 22 — helping them find paid work experience and build real career skills. I connect them with employers, navigate workforce programs, and sit with students who are motivated and capable but have no idea where to start.

The problem I kept running into wasn't effort. It was information. There was no single place a 209-area student could go to find out what was available. Government websites are outdated. School counselors are stretched thin. National job boards have nothing local. Students were missing deadlines for programs they'd never even heard of — programs they'd have been perfect for.

I've been in 209-area classrooms and programs since 2017. Before the DOR, I taught in the Stanislaus County school system and spent time as an academic teacher inside the CDCR juvenile justice facility in Stockton — working with some of the most overlooked young people in our region. That experience shaped everything about how I think about access to opportunity.

The students I work with — students with disabilities, students who've been incarcerated, first-generation students who don't have a network to tap — are exactly the students who get left behind when information is scattered and hard to find. Internships209 is my attempt to change that, one listing at a time.

This site is free. It will stay free. The students in the 209 don't need another barrier — they need a door that's actually open.

"The opportunity gap in the 209 is real. But so is the talent. Students here are ready — they just need someone to tell them where the door is."

— Baris Kourdou, Founder

The Site's Commitments

What this site stands for — and what it will never compromise on.

🆓

Free for Every Student

No accounts. No fees. No paywall. Every listing and every resource on this site is free for every student, period. That will never change.

✅

Real, Verified Listings

Every listing is checked before it goes up. No ghost postings, no spam, no listings that lead nowhere. Just real opportunities from real organizations.

🌎

Bilingual & Accessible

The site is available in English and Spanish. The 209 is a bilingual community — this resource should be too.

🔄

Actively Maintained

Listings are updated regularly. Expired opportunities are removed. New ones are added as they come in. This is a living resource, not a snapshot.
